The Golden Age of Egyptian Music (1940s-1970s) was considered the most iconic era of Egyptian Music. Where Classical Music also known as 'Tarab' or 'طرب' was at its peak, with millions of listeners globally.

Classical Music produced legendary singers and composers with shining legacies, that are impactful to this day.

Key Figures that Shaped Egyptian Music:

  • Umm Kulthum (1898-1975) - Known as "The Star of the East" and was the most revered singer in the Middle East of all time. Captivating the audience with her emotional voice and poetic songs. She was praised globally with millions queuing for her shows in Cairo, Paris and more.
  • Mohammed Abdel Wahab (1902-1991) - A singer and mainly a composer that bledned the Arabic scales with western influences, pivoting many new styles and becoming an icon in the Middle East.
  • Abdel Halim Hafez (1929-1977) - Known as "The Nightingale" who was famous for his deep voice and romantic songs, which captivated younger audiences across the Middle East.
  • Sherine Abdelwahab (1980-Present) - Known as "صوت مصر" which is translated as "the Sound of Egypt", her emotional depth resonates with her audience and she cemented her status by becoming the first Arab artist to be honoured at the Billboard Women in Music Awards. As well as, gaining a Guinness World Record for maintaining the top spot in Billboard Arabic 100 for 11 consecutive weeks.
  • Wegz (1998-Present) - His music fuses EDM trap, shaabi, and pop to reflect the diverse contemporary music scence in Egypt. He was named as Spotify's most-streamed Arab Artist in the MENA region and cemented his status further by performing at the 2022 FIFA World Cup. As well as, featuring in Forbes' 30 Under 30 in 2023.

The Golden Age of Egyptian Cinema (1940s-1960s) was considered the peak of Egyptian Cinema. Cairo was then considered "The Hollywood of the East" with hundreds of films produced annually.

Many genres were produced but they all conveyed a rich story and contained elements of emotional drama that resonated with its audience. Soon enough, the Egyptian Dialect was the standard for the MENA region and an example of this was Walt Disney choosing the Egyptian Dialect for his Arabic Versions of his cartoon films. This was due to the Egyptians perfecting communication that entailed an emotion, portraying love, humuour and other emotions excellently.

It is known that to this day, for an actor to grow in the MENA region, they should work in an Egyptian Production. As Egyptian Arabic has become the standard, popular and comfort choice amongst the audience of the region centuries ago.

Key Figues that Shaped Egyptian Cinema:

  • Ismail Yassin (1912-1972) - Known as "The King of Comedy and slapstick humour" and became a part of Egypt's legacy in comedic cinema.
  • Faten Hamama (1931-2015) - Known as "Lady of the Arabic Screen who starred in over 90 films and collaborated with many legendary directors like Youssef Chahine.
  • Omar Sharif (1932-2015) - One of the most inernationally recognised actors, and gained global fame through Hollywood Movies like "Lawrence of Arabia". He often partnered with Faten Hamama and together were the iconic cinema couple for a period of time.
  • Soad Hosny (1943-2001) - Known as the "Cinderella of Egyptian Cinema" as she was often admired for her natural charisma, elegance and beauty in the Arab world. Who also starred in many diverse roles in Romantic Dramas and Social Realism films.
  • Ahmed Zaki (1949-2005) - Known as the "Black Tiger" as he possessed powerful acting skills and the ability to portray complex characters. Also famous for many biographical roles like late presidents and became a very influential actor in Egyptian Cinema.
